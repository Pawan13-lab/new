Active Listening

Unlock Deeper Connections

Definition & Impact

What Is Active Listening?

Fully concentrating, understanding, and responding thoughtfully

Why It Matters:

Builds trust & respect

Creates foundation for meaningful relationships

Prevents miscommunication

Reduces errors and misunderstandings

Fosters empathy & collaboration

Strengthens team dynamics

Drives better outcomes

Improves decision-making and results

Core Skills

Skill What It Looks Like
Attention Facing speaker, maintaining eye contact
Reflection Paraphrasing: "You're saying…"
Clarification Asking open questions: "Can you explain…?"
Summarization Recapping key points at the end
Nonverbal Cues Nodding, appropriate facial expressions

Common Barriers

Distractions
Misses details
Quick Fix: Put devices away, choose quiet spaces
Interrupting
Silences speaker
Quick Fix: Wait 2-3 seconds before replying
Prejudgment
Biased interpretations
Quick Fix: Notice assumptions, stay curious
Reactivity
Shifts focus inward
Quick Fix: Pause, breathe, reset
Overload
Mental fatigue
Quick Fix: Take notes, ask for breaks

strategies for the mastery

Making the eye contact
show attentiveness by looking at the speaker
Use open body language:
keep your posture open and inviting.Avoiding the cross arm
Provide verbal affirmation
use the small cues like "i see" or "go on" to encourage the speaker
Paraphrase and summarize
Repeat back what the speaker has said in your own words to confirm understanding
Ask Open-end Question
  • Encourage deeper discussion by asking questions that cannot be answered with a simple "yes" or "no."
  • Allow the speaker time to express themselves without rushing or finishing their sentences.

Other strategies

Avoid Interrupting:

Let the speaker finish their thoughts before responding. This shows respect for their message

Reflect Emotions

Acknowledge the speaker's feelings by stating things like, “It sounds like you’re feeling…”

Limit Distractions:

Find a quiet environment and put away devices to focus entirely on the conversation.

Practice Empathy

Try to understand the speaker’s perspective and emotions, fostering a deeper connection.
